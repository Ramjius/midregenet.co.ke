Welcome to Midre Genet

A culinary journey to the vibrant flavors of Ethiopian and Eritrean cuisine. At Midre Genet, we invite you to experience the rich and diverse culinary traditions of East Africa.

Our restaurant is named after "Midre Genet," which translates to "Paradise" in Amharic, one of the languages spoken in Ethiopia and Eritrea. We aim to create a little piece of paradise right here, where you can indulge in the authentic tastes and warm hospitality of our vibrant cultures.

Our menu is a reflection of the centuries-old culinary heritage of Ethiopia and Eritrea. We meticulously craft each dish, paying homage to traditional recipes passed down through generations. From the fiery spices of our wats and the tangy flavors of injera to the aromatic coffee that graces our tables, every bite and sip is designed to transport you to the heart of East Africa. Join us at Midre Genet and embark on a memorable adventure through the vibrant flavors, spices, and traditions of Ethiopia and Eritrea. We look forward to serving you and sharing the magic of our cuisine in every dish we prepare.

Ethiopian Coffee (Buna) Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coarsely ground Ethiopian coffee beans
  • 3 cups water
  • Sugar (optional)
Instructions:

Bring the water to a boil in a traditional Ethiopian coffee pot called a jebena or a regular pot. Add the coarsely ground coffee to the boiling water and let it simmer for a few minutes. The coffee grounds will gradually sink to the bottom. Remove the pot from heat and let it sit for a minute or two to allow the coffee grounds to settle completely. Place a fine-mesh sieve or a coffee filter over a coffee pot or a carafe. Slowly pour the brewed coffee through the sieve, straining out the grounds. Serve the rich and aromatic Ethiopian coffee in small cups, traditionally accompanied by a small bowl of sugar on the side. Enjoy the coffee sip by sip, savoring its bold flavors and engaging in lively conversation.

Eritrean Himbasha Recipe

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup semolina
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup melted butter or vegetable oil
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 cup honey (for drizzling)
Instructions:

Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a square baking dish or line it with parchment paper. In a large bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, semolina, sugar, baking powder, cardamom, cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg. Mix well to combine the dry ingredients. Add the melted butter or vegetable oil to the dry ingredients and mix until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs. Slowly add the water to the mixture, a little at a time, while stirring. Mix until the butter becomes smooth and pourable. Pour the butter into the prepared baking dish

Injera

Ingredients:
  • 2 cups teff flour
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast (optional)
  • Salt to taste
Instructions:

In a large bowl, combine the teff flour and water. Mix well until a smooth batter is formed. If desired, you can add yeast to aid in fermentation. Cover the bowl with a cloth and let it sit at room temperature for at least 24 hours, allowing the batter to ferment. After fermentation, the batter should have a slightly sour smell and small bubbles on the surface. Stir the batter and add salt to taste. Heat a non-stick skillet or a large frying pan over medium heat. Pour a ladleful of the batter onto the skillet, spreading it in a circular motion to form a thin, crepe-like pancake. Cover the skillet and let the injera cook for a few minutes until bubbles form on the surface and the edges start to lift. The injera should be cooked only on one side and remain soft and spongy. Remove the injera from the skillet and let it cool. Repeat the process with the remaining batter, stacking the injera on a plate. Serve the injera with various stews, curries, or vegetable dishes. Tear off a piece of injera and use it to scoop up the other dishes, using it as a utensil and a delicious accompaniment to the meal.
